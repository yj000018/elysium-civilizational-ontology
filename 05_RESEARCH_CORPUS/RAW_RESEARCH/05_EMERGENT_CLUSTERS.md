# 05 — Emergent Clusters

## Methodology

Rather than forcing the provisional 7-layer structure, this analysis lets the 111 models reveal their own clustering patterns. Clusters emerge from convergence across multiple independent traditions and frameworks.

---

## Cluster 1: Material Foundation / Physical Base

**Domains included:** body/survival, energy, water, habitat, infrastructure, materials, mobility

**Models strongly supporting:** Planetary Boundaries, Doughnut Economics, SDGs, Worldchanging, Permaculture, Circular Economy, Cradle to Cradle, Natural Step, Energy Transition Models, Smart Cities

**Models weakly supporting:** Maslow (physiological needs), Chakra system (root), Ayurveda (physical body)

**Primary scale:** Society → Civilization → Planet

**Why this cluster matters:** Every civilization requires a material substrate. Without energy, water, shelter, and infrastructure, no higher function is possible. This is the most universally acknowledged foundation across all frameworks.

**Arguments for inclusion:** Appears in 90%+ of models either directly or implicitly. Universal prerequisite.

**Arguments against inclusion:** Risk of being too broad (energy vs. water vs. infrastructure are very different domains).

**Possible overlap:** With Life/Vitality (food, health overlap with material base)

**Should it be:** Foundation (Layer 1)

**Confidence score:** 5/5

---

## Cluster 2: Life / Vitality / Health / Regeneration

**Domains included:** food, health, biodiversity, ecology, agriculture, regeneration, biological systems

**Models strongly supporting:** Permaculture, Biomimicry, Gaia Theory, Ecosystem Services, One Health, Regenerative Design, Deep Ecology, Ecological Economics, Indigenous Ecological Knowledge, Ayurveda, SDGs (Goals 2,3,14,15)

**Models weakly supporting:** Maslow (safety/physiological), Chakra (sacral), Doughnut Economics (ecological ceiling)

**Primary scale:** Planet → Society → Individual

**Why this cluster matters:** Life itself — biological vitality, health, food systems, biodiversity — forms a distinct layer between pure material infrastructure and human agency. It represents the living substrate upon which civilization depends.

**Arguments for inclusion:** Distinct from inert material base. Strongly supported by ecological models. Increasingly critical in the Anthropocene.

**Arguments against inclusion:** Could be merged with Material Foundation in a simpler model.

**Possible overlap:** Material Foundation (energy from biomass), Connection/Cohesion (ecological relationships)

**Should it be:** Foundation (Layer 2)

**Confidence score:** 5/5

---

## Cluster 3: Agency / Economy / Production / Power

**Domains included:** economy, work, value creation, entrepreneurship, finance, innovation, technology, production

**Models strongly supporting:** Capitalism/Post-capitalism, Carlota Perez, Rifkin, Mazzucato, Platform Cooperativism, Industry 4.0, Ostrom (commons governance), Degrowth, Open-Source Economy, Future of Work, UBI/UBS

**Models weakly supporting:** Maslow (esteem), Spiral Dynamics (orange), Chakra (solar plexus), Self-Determination Theory (competence)

**Primary scale:** Individual → Society → Civilization

**Why this cluster matters:** The capacity to act, produce, create value, and transform material conditions. This is where human agency meets economic systems. It encompasses both individual entrepreneurship and collective economic organization.

**Arguments for inclusion:** Universally present. Clear distinction from material base (which is passive) and from governance (which is coordination).

**Arguments against inclusion:** Technology could be its own cluster. Finance vs. work vs. innovation are very different.

**Possible overlap:** Expression/Governance (economic governance), Knowledge/Vision (innovation requires knowledge)

**Should it be:** Foundation (Layer 3)

**Confidence score:** 5/5

---

## Cluster 4: Connection / Community / Culture / Cohesion

**Domains included:** community, relationships, family, culture, art, social capital, trust, solidarity, identity, narrative

**Models strongly supporting:** Ubuntu, Indigenous Worldviews, Confucianism, Ibn Khaldun (asabiyyah), World Values Survey, Social Progress Index, PERMA (relationships), SDT (relatedness), Campbell (collective narrative), IDGs

**Models weakly supporting:** Senge (shared vision), Theory U (social field), Erikson (intimacy/generativity), Positive Psychology

**Primary scale:** Individual → Community → Society

**Why this cluster matters:** The social fabric — relationships, culture, art, collective identity, trust — is what holds societies together. Without cohesion, no governance or knowledge system can function. This is the "glue" of civilization.

**Arguments for inclusion:** Extremely strong support (83 models rate it strongly). Distinct from governance (which is formal) and from economy (which is transactional).

**Arguments against inclusion:** Culture and community are very broad. Art could be in Expression.

**Possible overlap:** Expression/Governance (media, communication bridge both), Consciousness/Meaning (culture carries meaning)

**Should it be:** Foundation (Layer 4)

**Confidence score:** 5/5

---

## Cluster 5: Expression / Governance / Coordination

**Domains included:** governance, law/justice, democracy, communication, media, language, coordination, security, institutions

**Models strongly supporting:** Earth Charter, DAO governance, Viable System Model, Transition Management, Ostrom (polycentric governance), Confucianism (li/ritual), Plato (ideal city), Islamic Maqasid, Christian Social Teaching (subsidiarity)

**Models weakly supporting:** Cybernetics (control/communication), Agile/Lean (organizational coordination), Spiral Dynamics (blue/orange institutions)

**Primary scale:** Society → Civilization

**Why this cluster matters:** How societies organize, coordinate, make decisions, resolve conflicts, and communicate. This is the structural architecture of collective action — distinct from the content (culture) and the purpose (meaning).

**Arguments for inclusion:** Governance + communication + law form a coherent functional cluster. Present in most civilization-scale models.

**Arguments against inclusion:** Communication could belong to Connection. Security could belong to Material Foundation.

**Possible overlap:** Connection (communication), Agency (economic governance), Knowledge (science policy)

**Should it be:** Foundation (Layer 5)

**Confidence score:** 4/5

---

## Cluster 6: Knowledge / Vision / Education / Science

**Domains included:** education, science, research, technology (as knowledge), philosophy, foresight, wisdom, systems thinking

**Models strongly supporting:** Piaget, Kohlberg, Kegan (cognitive development), Integral Theory (vision-logic), Systems Theory, Cybernetics, Buckminster Fuller, Club of Rome (modeling), SDGs (Goal 4), Theory U (sensing/seeing)

**Models weakly supporting:** Buddhism (right view), Vedanta (jnana/knowledge), Stoicism (wisdom), Fowler (faith as knowing)

**Primary scale:** Individual → Society → Civilization

**Why this cluster matters:** The capacity to understand, learn, research, model, and foresee. This is civilization's "nervous system" — its ability to perceive reality accurately and anticipate futures.

**Arguments for inclusion:** Distinct from governance (knowing vs. deciding) and from meaning (understanding vs. purpose). Strongly supported across developmental and systems models.

**Arguments against inclusion:** Technology straddles this and Agency. Wisdom straddles this and Consciousness.

**Possible overlap:** Agency (applied knowledge = innovation), Consciousness (wisdom, philosophy)

**Should it be:** Foundation (Layer 6)

**Confidence score:** 4/5

---

## Cluster 7: Consciousness / Meaning / Purpose / Ethics

**Domains included:** ethics, meaning, spirituality, consciousness, civilizational purpose, planetary direction, values, worldview

**Models strongly supporting:** Spiral Dynamics (turquoise), Integral Theory (causal/nondual), Vedanta, Buddhism, Taoism, Fowler (universalizing faith), Cook-Greuter (unitive), Deep Ecology (intrinsic value), Earth Charter (ethical principles), Metamodern models

**Models weakly supporting:** Maslow (self-transcendence), PERMA (meaning), Erikson (integrity), Stoicism (virtue/logos)

**Primary scale:** Individual → Civilization → Universal

**Why this cluster matters:** The orienting layer — what gives direction and purpose to all other layers. Without meaning, ethics, and consciousness, civilization has no compass. This is the "why" that guides the "how."

**Arguments for inclusion:** Present in all wisdom traditions. Increasingly recognized in modern frameworks (IDGs, metamodern). Distinct from knowledge (purpose vs. understanding).

**Arguments against inclusion:** Hardest to operationalize. Risk of new-age drift. Some argue it should be distributed across all layers rather than being its own.

**Possible overlap:** Knowledge (philosophy), Connection (cultural meaning)

**Should it be:** Foundation (Layer 7)

**Confidence score:** 4/5

## Robust Clusters (confirmed)
All 7 clusters show strong convergence across models.

## Ambiguous Boundaries
- Technology: straddles Agency (applied) and Knowledge (theoretical)
- Communication: straddles Connection (social) and Governance (institutional)
- Wisdom: straddles Knowledge (understanding) and Consciousness (purpose)
- Art: straddles Connection (cultural expression) and Consciousness (meaning-making)

## Clusters That Could Be Merged
- Material Foundation + Life/Vitality → "Physical/Biological Base" (but loses important distinction)
- Knowledge + Consciousness → "Mind/Wisdom" (but loses important distinction between knowing and meaning)

## Clusters That Should Remain Separate
- All 7 clusters have sufficient independent support to justify separation
- The material→vital→agency→connection→expression→knowledge→consciousness progression is supported by multiple independent traditions

## Verdict
**The 7-layer hypothesis is CONFIRMED with high confidence.** The provisional structure maps remarkably well onto emergent patterns from 111 diverse models. Minor naming adjustments recommended.
