# 08 — Taxonomy Levels

## Purpose

Define clear hierarchical levels for the Elysium ontology, distinguishing between scales, foundations, facets, sub-facets, examples, and resources.

---

## Level Definitions

### Scale (Meta-level)
The level of observation/application.

**Criteria:**
- Represents a fundamentally different level of organization
- Each scale has its own dynamics, actors, and timescales
- The same foundations apply at each scale (fractal principle)

**The 3 Scales:**
1. **Human** — Individual, personal, interior
2. **Society** — Collective, institutional, communal
3. **Civilization** — Planetary, species-level, epochal

---

### Foundation / Layer (Level 1)
Grand ontological level. The deepest structural category.

**Criteria:**
- Universal — exists in every civilization, past and present
- Fractal — manifests at all three scales
- Indispensable — civilization cannot function without it
- Autonomous — can be analyzed independently
- Evolutionary — carries a logic of development/progression
- Contains multiple facets
- Supported by majority of analyzed models

**The 7 Foundations:**
1. **Foundation** — Material base, infrastructure, resources, energy
2. **Vitality** — Life, health, food, biodiversity, regeneration
3. **Agency** — Economy, work, production, innovation, value creation
4. **Cohesion** — Community, relationships, culture, art, social fabric
5. **Governance** — Law, coordination, communication, institutions, democracy
6. **Vision** — Knowledge, education, science, foresight, systems thinking
7. **Consciousness** — Ethics, meaning, purpose, spirituality, civilizational direction

---

### Facet / Domain (Level 2)
Major component within a foundation.

**Criteria:**
- Concrete enough to be a chapter topic
- Can be transformed by technology, policy, or consciousness shift
- Not universal enough to be its own foundation
- Clearly belongs to one primary foundation (though may touch others)
- Can have its own "Today → Crisis → Transformation → Future" narrative

**Examples by Foundation:**

| Foundation | Key Facets |
|-----------|-----------|
| Foundation | Energy, Water, Habitat, Infrastructure, Mobility, Materials |
| Vitality | Health, Food/Agriculture, Biodiversity, Ecology, Regeneration |
| Agency | Economy, Work, Finance, Entrepreneurship, Innovation, Technology |
| Cohesion | Community, Relationships, Culture, Art, Media, Identity |
| Governance | Law/Justice, Democracy, Communication, Security, Institutions |
| Vision | Education, Science, Research, Foresight, Philosophy, Systems Thinking |
| Consciousness | Ethics, Spirituality, Purpose, Values, Worldview, Civilizational Direction |

---

### Sub-facet (Level 3)
Specific component within a facet.

**Criteria:**
- Too specific for a chapter but important for completeness
- Represents a concrete area of practice or knowledge
- Can be the subject of a project, policy, or innovation

**Examples:**
- Energy → Solar energy, Smart grids, Nuclear fusion, Energy storage
- Health → Mental health, Preventive medicine, Traditional medicine, Longevity
- Governance → Participatory budgeting, Digital democracy, Restorative justice

---

### Example / Case Study (Level 4)
Concrete project, initiative, place, or application.

**Criteria:**
- Specific, named, locatable
- Demonstrates a principle in action
- Time-bound or place-bound
- Illustrative, not definitional

**Examples:**
- EcoHouse Brazil, Barefoot College, Portland, Barcelona Superblocks
- Mondragon Cooperative, Wikipedia, Costa Rica reforestation

---

### Resource (Level 5)
Book, tool, organization, dataset, method, or technology that supports understanding or action.

**Criteria:**
- External to the ontology itself
- Useful for learning, measuring, or implementing
- Can be recommended to users

**Examples:**
- Books: Doughnut Economics, Thinking in Systems, Braiding Sweetgrass
- Organizations: IPCC, Ellen MacArthur Foundation, WEAll
- Tools: OpenStreetMap, SDG Tracker, Our World in Data
- Methods: Theory U, Permaculture design, Systems mapping

---

## Naming Conventions

| Level | Naming Style | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Scale | Single word, capitalized | Human, Society, Civilization |
| Foundation | Single evocative word | Foundation, Vitality, Agency, Cohesion, Governance, Vision, Consciousness |
| Facet | 1-2 words, concrete | Energy, Public Health, Democracy |
| Sub-facet | 2-4 words, specific | Solar energy storage, Participatory budgeting |
| Example | Proper noun or project name | Barefoot College, Barcelona Superblocks |
| Resource | Full title + type | "Doughnut Economics" (book), IPCC (organization) |
