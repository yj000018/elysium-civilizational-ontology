# 17 — How This Ontology Was Built

*A chapter on methodology, intellectual adventure, and gratitude.*

---

## The Adventurer's Approach

This ontology was not designed in an armchair. It was discovered through an adventurous, empirical, almost archaeological process — digging through 126 models, frameworks, and traditions from radically different disciplines, eras, and cultures, searching for the hidden structure beneath them all.

The approach was deliberately **non-academic** in spirit. No single school of thought was taken as gospel. No pre-existing ontology was adopted wholesale. Instead, the method was closer to that of a naturalist cataloging species in an unexplored territory: observe everything, classify nothing prematurely, and let the patterns emerge from the data itself.

This is not a synthesis born of theory. It is a synthesis born of immersion.

---

## The Method: Systematic Extraction of Commonalities

The construction of Elysium followed a rigorous but open-ended methodology:

**Step 1 — Casting the widest possible net.** Models were selected from developmental psychology (Maslow, Graves, Kegan), systems theory (Meadows, Luhmann, Capra), ecology (Holling, Wahl, Macy), economics (Raworth, Mazzucato, Graeber), philosophy (Wilber, Morin, Bhaskar), wisdom traditions (Aurobindo, Buddhism, Indigenous knowledge), urbanism (Jacobs, Gehl, Alexander), peace studies (Galtung), pedagogy (Freire), and dozens more. The only criterion: does this model attempt to map some dimension of human or civilizational reality?

**Step 2 — Structured analysis of each model.** Every model was subjected to the same analytical protocol: What does it explicitly name? What does it implicitly assume? What scale does it operate at? What domains does it cover? What does it miss? This produced 126 structured "model cards" — each a standardized extraction of the model's ontological DNA.

**Step 3 — Cross-comparison and pattern detection.** The 126 cards were then compared across 38 civilizational domains. A matrix was built. Frequency analysis revealed which domains appeared most often, which were systematically neglected, and which clusters of domains tended to co-occur.

**Step 4 — Emergence of the 7 Foundations.** The data spoke clearly. Seven clusters emerged with remarkable consistency — not because they were imposed, but because model after model, tradition after tradition, independently converged on the same layered structure: a progression from material base, through life, agency, social fabric, coordination, knowledge, to meaning.

**Step 5 — Validation through stress-testing.** The provisional 7-layer structure was then tested against the Worldchanging catalog (100% coverage achieved), against the fractal hypothesis (does it hold at Human, Society, and Civilization scales? — yes, score 4.7/5), and against gap analysis (which led to the addition of 15 missing models in Lot H).

## The Écolier Spirit

There is something of the eternal student in this approach — the *écolier* who sits at the feet of 126 masters, not to become a disciple of any one, but to hear what they all say when their voices are layered together.

Maslow spoke of needs. Graves spoke of values. Meadows spoke of leverage points. Ostrom spoke of commons. Morin spoke of complexity. Aurobindo spoke of consciousness. Raworth spoke of boundaries. Capra spoke of webs. Galtung spoke of peace. Freire spoke of liberation. Alexander spoke of living structure.

Each saw a piece. The ontology is the attempt to see the whole — not by being smarter than any of them, but by being more patient, more systematic, and more willing to let the structure reveal itself.

## What This Process Revealed

The most striking discovery was not any single model's brilliance — it was the **convergence**. When you lay 126 independent attempts to map reality side by side, the same deep structure appears again and again:

1. A material/physical base that must be secured first.
2. A living/biological layer that sustains and regenerates.
3. A productive/economic layer that transforms and creates.
4. A social/cultural layer that connects and coheres.
5. A coordinative/governance layer that organizes and resolves.
6. A cognitive/knowledge layer that understands and anticipates.
7. A meaning/consciousness layer that orients and transcends.

No single thinker invented this structure. It was already there — implicit in Maslow, in Spiral Dynamics, in the SDGs, in Buddhist dependent origination, in Panarchy, in Planetary Boundaries. The contribution of Elysium is not invention but **recognition** — making explicit what was always implicit, giving it a single coherent name and architecture.

## Limitations and Humility

This ontology is a map, not the territory. It is necessarily a simplification. Some important caveats:

The 7-layer structure privileges a **vertical/hierarchical** reading. Reality is also horizontal, rhizomatic, and paradoxical. The fractal property helps (each layer contains all others), but the linear numbering can mislead.

The model selection, while broad (126), is still biased toward **Western, English-language, academic** sources. Indigenous, oral, and non-textual traditions are underrepresented despite efforts to include them.

The extraction process relied on **AI-assisted analysis** — powerful for pattern detection at scale, but potentially smoothing over tensions, contradictions, and incommensurabilities between traditions that a human scholar would preserve.

This is version 2.0. It will evolve.
