## Gap Analysis

### What Worldchanging covers that Elysium covers well:
- Material infrastructure (energy, water, habitat, mobility) — excellent
- Ecological systems (biodiversity, ecosystems, regeneration) — excellent
- Economic innovation (entrepreneurship, finance, production) — excellent
- Political action (democracy, rights, transparency) — excellent

### What Worldchanging covers weakly that Elysium covers strongly:
- **Consciousness / Meaning / Spirituality** — Worldchanging barely touches this (only 2.7%). Elysium's 7th Foundation is a major structural addition.
- **Identity / Culture / Art** — Worldchanging treats these peripherally. Elysium gives them full Facet status.
- **Systems Thinking / Foresight** — Worldchanging implies but doesn't structure these. Elysium makes them explicit.

### What Worldchanging covers that could strengthen Elysium:
- **Refugees / Humanitarian crisis** — Currently mapped to Sécurité, but could deserve stronger presence as a cross-cutting theme
- **Leapfrogging** — A powerful concept for Global South development; currently under Technologie but could be a cross-Foundation principle
- **Place-making** — At the intersection of Community, Habitat, and Culture; confirms the need for cross-Facet bridges

### Missing from BOTH Worldchanging and Elysium:
- Nothing critical identified. Elysium's scope exceeds Worldchanging's by including consciousness, spirituality, worldview, and civilizational direction.

---

## Conclusion

**Elysium achieves 100% coverage of Worldchanging's 111 sub-topics** while adding significant dimensions that Worldchanging does not address (consciousness, spirituality, worldview, civilizational direction, identity, systems thinking as explicit layers).

The audit confirms that Elysium is a strict superset of Worldchanging's ontological scope. No architectural changes required.
