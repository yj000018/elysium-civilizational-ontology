# Elysium Ontology — Facets Definition

Based on the 126-model research and Worldchanging gap analysis, here are the finalized 38 Facets organized under the 7 Foundations.

## 1. Foundation (Material Base)
*The physical substrate and infrastructure required for civilization to exist.*
- **Energy:** Capture, storage, and distribution of power (renewables, grids, efficiency).
- **Water:** Access, conservation, and management of fresh water.
- **Habitat:** Shelter, housing, green building, and compact living.
- **Infrastructure:** Urban systems, smart cities, and physical networks.
- **Mobility:** Transportation, logistics, and transit systems.
- **Materials:** Resource extraction, circularity, and green materials.

## 2. Vitality (Life & Ecology)
*The biological and ecological systems that sustain living beings and the planet.*
- **Health:** Public health, personal vitality, and holistic well-being.
- **Food & Agriculture:** Sustainable food systems, regenerative agriculture, and nutrition.
- **Biodiversity:** Species preservation, habitat protection, and ecological diversity.
- **Ecosystems:** Bioregions, oceans, forests, and planetary boundaries.
- **Regeneration:** Restoration ecology, healing polluted land, and planetary healing.

## 3. Agency (Economy & Production)
*The capacity to act, produce, create value, and transform material conditions.*
- **Economy:** Macro-economic models, post-capitalism, and value exchange.
- **Work:** Labor, employment, future of work, and livelihoods.
- **Finance:** Capital allocation, microfinance, and investment.
- **Entrepreneurship:** Startups, social business, and organizational design.
- **Innovation:** Technological development, R&D, and open source.
- **Production:** Manufacturing, industry, and the maker movement.

## 4. Cohesion (Community & Culture)
*The social fabric, relationships, and shared identities that bind people together.*
- **Community:** Local networks, social capital, and civic engagement.
- **Relationships:** Family, interpersonal bonds, and emotional connection.
- **Culture:** Shared narratives, traditions, and global exchange.
- **Art:** Creative expression, aesthetics, and cultural production.
- **Media:** Information flows, journalism, and public discourse.
- **Identity:** Belonging, inclusion, and cultural evolution.

## 5. Governance (Coordination & Law)
*The structures, laws, and institutions through which societies coordinate and resolve conflict.*
- **Law & Justice:** Legal frameworks, human rights, and restorative justice.
- **Democracy:** Political participation, voting, and civic voice.
- **Institutions:** Public administration, governance models, and polycentricity.
- **Security:** Peace studies, nonviolence, and conflict resolution.
- **Communication:** Tools for coordination, transparency, and dialogue.

## 6. Vision (Knowledge & Foresight)
*The capacity to understand reality, learn, and anticipate the future.*
- **Education:** Learning systems, literacy, and critical pedagogy.
- **Science:** Research, empiricism, and collective intelligence.
- **Foresight:** Futures thinking, climate modeling, and long-term planning.
- **Systems Thinking:** Understanding complexity, feedback loops, and holism.
- **Technology (as tool):** AI, computation, and tools for conviviality.

## 7. Consciousness (Meaning & Purpose)
*The orienting layer that provides direction, ethics, and meaning to civilization.*
- **Ethics:** Moral frameworks, values, and bioethics.
- **Spirituality:** Inner development, wisdom traditions, and transcendence.
- **Purpose:** Individual meaning and collective existential direction.
- **Worldview:** Paradigms, mental models, and ontological shifts.
- **Civilizational Direction:** The ultimate trajectory and "Great Turning" of humanity.
