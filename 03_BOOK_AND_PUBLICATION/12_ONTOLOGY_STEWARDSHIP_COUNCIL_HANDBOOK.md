## Definitions
- **7 Foundations:** The core principles forming the basis of ELYSIUM's ontology.
- **38 Facets:** The specific aspects or dimensions through which the Foundations are expressed and operationalized.

## 1. Introduction
The Ontology Stewardship Council (OSC) is the designated body responsible for the ongoing maintenance and evolution of ELYSIUM's 7 Foundations and 38 Facets. This document outlines the structure, responsibilities, and procedures of the OSC, ensuring that ELYSIUM's core ontology remains coherent, relevant, and adaptive to the evolving needs of the global community.

## 2. Structure of the Ontology Stewardship Council

### 2.1 Composition
- The OSC is composed of multidisciplinary experts, including ontologists, linguists, system architects, ethicists, and domain specialists.
- Membership is subject to a nomination and vetting process conducted by an independent advisory board.
- The council shall consist of no less than 15 and no more than 25 members to ensure diversity and balance of expertise.

### 2.2 Leadership
- The OSC is led by a Chairperson and a Vice-Chairperson, elected by council members for a two-year term.
- The Chairperson is responsible for facilitating meetings and representing the OSC in public forums.
- The Vice-Chairperson assists the Chairperson and steps in as acting Chairperson when necessary.

## 3. Responsibilities of the Ontology Stewardship Council

### 3.1 Maintenance of the 7 Foundations and 38 Facets
- The OSC is responsible for the continuous review and updating of the Foundations and Facets to reflect new insights, technologies, and societal changes.
- The council ensures alignment with ELYSIUM's principles of openness, inclusivity, sustainability, and ethical integrity.

### 3.2 Approval of Changes
- Proposals for changes can be submitted by any ELYSIUM stakeholder or member of the global community.
- Each proposal undergoes a preliminary assessment by a designated subcommittee to evaluate feasibility, impact, and alignment with the current ontology.
- Proposals passing the initial assessment are presented to the full council for deliberation.

## 4. Change Approval Process

### 4.1 Proposal Submission
- Proposals must be submitted in writing, detailing the proposed change, rationale, and expected impact on the Foundations and Facets.

### 4.2 Evaluation and Deliberation
- The subcommittee evaluates the proposal against criteria such as coherence, necessity, potential benefits, and ethical considerations.
- The full council deliberates on proposals during scheduled meetings, with input from relevant experts or stakeholders as needed.

### 4.3 Decision Making
- Decisions require a two-thirds majority vote of the council members present at the meeting, with a quorum of at least 60% of members required.

### 4.4 Communication and Implementation
- Approved changes are communicated to all stakeholders and the wider community through official ELYSIUM channels.
- The OSC coordinates with technical teams to implement changes in the systems and platforms utilizing the ontology.

## 5. Conflict-of-Interest Policy
- Members must disclose any potential conflicts of interest related to proposals or council activities.
- Members with conflicts must abstain from voting or deliberation on affected proposals.

## 6. Enhanced Community Involvement
- Mechanisms for active community engagement include public forums, feedback sessions, and open consultations on significant proposals.

## 7. Review and Amendment of the Handbook
- This handbook is subject to review and amendment by the OSC every three years or as needed to accommodate significant changes in practice or context.
- Amendments require the same approval process as changes to the Foundations and Facets.

## 8. Conclusion
The Ontology Stewardship Council plays a critical role in safeguarding the integrity and adaptability of ELYSIUM's core ontology. Through diligent stewardship, ethical oversight, and open collaboration, the OSC ensures that ELYSIUM remains a robust framework for fostering global understanding and cooperation.
