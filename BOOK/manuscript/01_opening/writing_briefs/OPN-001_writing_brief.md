## 6. Module Purpose

Open the book from lived experience. Invite the reader to recognize a feeling they already carry: the sense that the world is not merely changing, but losing coherence. Name this as civilizational — not personal, not sectoral, but systemic.

---

## 7. Opening Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Open with a concrete atmospheric vignette that evokes civilizational unease before naming it — a scene, a moment, a texture of contemporary life that the reader recognizes

---

## 8. Closing Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Close by naming the feeling as civilizational — not a personal failure, not a sectoral crisis, but a systemic signal — and by signaling that the book will take this seriously

---

## 9. Three Key Concepts to Deploy

1. **Civilizational unease:** The diffuse, pre-theoretical sense that the world is losing coherence — not crisis, not catastrophe, but something subtler and more pervasive
2. **Phenomenological opening:** Beginning from lived experience rather than theory — the reader's own felt sense before any analytical framework
3. **Systemic signal:** Individual feelings of unease are not personal failures but symptoms of a larger structural condition

---

## 10. Two Traps to Avoid

1. **Doom rhetoric:** Do not catastrophize or use crisis language — the module should feel recognizing, not alarming
2. **Crisis catalogue:** Do not list crises (climate, inequality, democracy, etc.) — this comes later; here we stay with the feeling

---

## 11. Tone / Register

Warm, recognizing, literary. The reader should feel seen, not lectured. Essayistic rather than academic.

---

## 12. Depth / Density

Light conceptual density. Atmospheric and experiential. The reader should not feel they are reading theory yet.

---

## 13. Narrative Beats

1. A concrete atmospheric vignette — a moment of contemporary life that carries the feeling
2. Recognition: this feeling is widespread, not personal
3. The feeling has a name: civilizational unease
4. This is not doom — it is a signal worth taking seriously
5. An implicit reading contract begins: the book will take this seriously
