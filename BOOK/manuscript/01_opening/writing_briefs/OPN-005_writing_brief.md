## 6. Module Purpose

Show that fragmented solutions displace problems across time, space, and domain rather than solving them. Three types of displacement: temporal, spatial, domain. Non-partisan concrete examples.

---

## 7. Opening Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Open with a concrete example of a 'successful' solution that displaced its problem — temporal, spatial, or domain displacement — before naming the mechanism

---

## 8. Closing Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Close by showing that displacement is a structural feature of fragmented response — and that it requires a map that can see across time, space, and domain simultaneously

---

## 9. Three Key Concepts to Deploy

1. **Temporal displacement:** Solutions that push problems into the future — short-term fixes that create long-term costs
2. **Spatial displacement:** Solutions that push problems to other places — local improvements that export problems elsewhere
3. **Domain displacement:** Solutions that push problems into other domains — economic fixes that create social problems, etc.

---

## 10. Two Traps to Avoid

1. **Partisan political examples:** Use non-partisan examples — infrastructure, agriculture, urban planning, medicine
2. **Abstract argument:** The displacement logic must be shown through concrete examples, not stated as a principle

---

## 11. Tone / Register

Analytical, concrete, non-partisan.

---

## 12. Depth / Density

Medium. Three types of displacement, each illustrated concretely. Do not over-theorize.
