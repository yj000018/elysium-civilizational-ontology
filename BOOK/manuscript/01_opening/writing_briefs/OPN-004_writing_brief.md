## 6. Module Purpose

Critique the silo structure of contemporary knowledge and response systems. Respect expertise while showing that fragmentation creates structural blindness. Prepare the displacement logic of OPN-005.

---

## 7. Opening Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Open by acknowledging the genuine value of specialist expertise — then show how the silo structure creates blindness that no individual specialist can see

---

## 8. Closing Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Close by naming structural blindness as the core problem — not lack of intelligence or expertise, but the architecture of fragmentation itself

---

## 9. Three Key Concepts to Deploy

1. **Structural blindness:** The blindness that emerges from the architecture of fragmentation itself — not from lack of expertise but from the silo structure
2. **Expertise vs. fragmentation:** Individual expertise (valuable) vs. the fragmented architecture that prevents integration (problematic)
3. **The map problem:** Specialist maps are accurate within their domain but cannot show what lies between domains

---

## 10. Two Traps to Avoid

1. **Anti-expertise rhetoric:** Do not critique expertise itself — critique the architecture of fragmentation. Respect specialists.
2. **Abstract argument without grounding:** Use concrete examples of how silo thinking produces blindness

---

## 11. Tone / Register

Respectful, analytical, structurally rigorous. Fair to experts while being clear about the structural problem.

---

## 12. Depth / Density

Medium-high. Precise without being academic.

---

## 13. Narrative Beats

1. Acknowledge the genuine value and achievement of specialist expertise
2. Show a concrete example of how silo thinking produces blindness
3. Name the structural mechanism: fragmentation creates gaps that no specialist can see
4. The problem is not intelligence or effort — it is the architecture
5. Close: structural blindness is the core problem; a new kind of map is needed
