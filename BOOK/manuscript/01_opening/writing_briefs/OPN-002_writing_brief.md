## 6. Module Purpose

Show that the multiple crises of the present are not independent but form a systemic pattern of interdependence. Prepare the patient metaphor of OPN-003 by establishing that we are dealing with one organism, not many separate ailments.

---

## 7. Opening Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Open by acknowledging that the reader likely sees many separate crises — but immediately begin to show their structural connections, not as a list but as a pattern

---

## 8. Closing Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Close by establishing that these are not separate problems requiring separate solutions — they form one systemic pattern, and this changes what kind of response is needed

---

## 9. Three Key Concepts to Deploy

1. **Systemic interdependence:** Contemporary crises are structurally connected — not coincidental but causally linked through shared civilizational foundations
2. **Pattern vs. list:** Seeing crises as a pattern (structural) vs. a list (additive) — the former requires a different kind of response
3. **Civilizational metabolism (first hint):** The crises are symptoms of a metabolism under stress, not isolated failures — first light introduction

---

## 10. Two Traps to Avoid

1. **Crisis catalogue:** Do not list crises one by one — show structural connections, not a survey of contemporary problems
2. **'Everything is connected' cliché:** Be specific about structural mechanisms of interdependence — not vague claims

---

## 11. Tone / Register

Analytical but accessible. More structured than OPN-001 but not yet academic. The reader should feel the argument building.

---

## 12. Depth / Density

Medium conceptual density. Concrete examples of interdependence rather than abstract claims.

---

## 13. Narrative Beats

1. Acknowledge the reader's experience of multiple crises
2. Show two or three specific structural connections between crises
3. Name the pattern: these are not separate — they share structural roots
4. The patient metaphor begins to emerge: one organism, multiple symptoms
5. Close: a different kind of response is required — crisis-by-crisis management is insufficient
