## 6. Module Purpose

Reject the machine metaphor for civilization without being anti-technology. Show why the machine frame fails to capture civilizational dynamics. Prepare OPN-008's metabolic frame. Keep short.

---

## 7. Opening Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Open by naming the machine metaphor as the dominant frame for understanding civilization and organizations — then immediately begin to show its structural limits

---

## 8. Closing Direction

> **Directional only — not a finished sentence.**

Close by establishing that civilization is not a machine — it is something else, something living — and that OPN-008 will name what that something is

---

## 9. Three Key Concepts to Deploy

1. **The machine metaphor:** The dominant frame for civilization: inputs → outputs, optimization, control, parts replaceable — and why it fails
2. **Structural limits of the machine frame:** Machines do not self-organize, do not heal, do not evolve — civilization does all three
3. **The living-systems alternative:** Not anti-technology — but civilization requires a living-systems frame, not a mechanical one

---

## 10. Two Traps to Avoid

1. **Anti-technology rhetoric:** Reject the machine metaphor for civilization, not technology itself — be precise about the distinction
2. **Over-length:** Keep this module short — it is a clearing operation, not a full argument. OPN-008 carries the weight

---

## 11. Tone / Register

Crisp, precise, slightly provocative. The reader should feel the machine metaphor crumbling.

## 12. Depth / Density
