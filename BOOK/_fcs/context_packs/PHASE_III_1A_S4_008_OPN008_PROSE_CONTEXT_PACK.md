## 6. Five Classes of Civilizational Flows (introduce lightly — NOT develop)
1. **Energy flows** — the power that enables all civilizational activity
2. **Material flows** — the physical substrates civilization extracts, transforms, circulates, and discards
3. **Biological flows** — food, health, bodies, ecosystems, regeneration, and life-support systems
4. **Information flows** — knowledge, perception, signals, education, science, media, and foresight
5. **Meaning flows** — purpose, legitimacy, identity, ethics, narratives, and civilizational direction

These are **flow classes**, not the Seven Foundations. Make this distinction clear in the prose.

## 7. Chief Architect Opening Direction (orientation, not mandatory wording)
> A machine can be repaired from the outside. A living system must be understood from within its flows.
>
> If civilization is not merely built but grown, not merely operated but metabolized, then the central question changes. We are no longer asking how to make the machine more efficient. We are asking whether the organism remains viable.

## 8. Chief Architect Closing Direction (orientation, not mandatory wording)
> Once civilization is seen metabolically, crisis no longer appears as isolated malfunction. It appears as disturbed flow, failed regulation, accumulated waste, and lost orientation. The next task is therefore not to repair a machine part by part, but to learn how to read the metabolism as a whole.
