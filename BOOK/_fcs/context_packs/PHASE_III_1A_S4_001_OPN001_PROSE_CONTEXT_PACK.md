## 3. OPN-001 Writing Brief — Full Text

### Module Identity

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Module ID | OPN-001 |
| Title EN | The Felt Sense of Civilizational Malaise |
| Title FR | Le sentiment vécu du malaise civilisationnel |
| Movement | MOV-I — The Malaise of the Present |
| Part | 01_opening |
| Word Target | 600 words (min: 500 / max: 750) |
| Chief Architect Decision | APPROVE — READY |

### Opening Direction

Open with the reader's felt sense of civilizational malaise — intimate, slow, precise — without naming crises, without data, without solutions.

### Closing Direction

Close by establishing that the feeling is real, shared, and civilizational — and that naming it is the first step of the book's inquiry.

### Reader Transition

| | EN |
|---|---|
| **State In** | Diffuse unease; vague sense that something is wrong but no clear frame. |
| **State Out** | Named unease; the feeling is legitimate, shared, and civilizational in scope. |

### Beats

1. Open with a concrete atmospheric vignette (not a list of crises)
2. Name the feeling before naming the cause
3. Distinguish personal anxiety from civilizational perception
4. Establish: this is not pessimism, it is diagnosis
5. Close: the feeling is real, and it has a name

### Key Points to Develop

- Lived atmosphere before theory
- Crisis as loss of coherence, not just accumulation of problems
- The feeling is civilizational, not personal
- Avoid apocalypse rhetoric — this is not a doom narrative
- Prepare systemic diagnosis (OPN-002)

### Tone

- Intimate
- Slow
- Precise
- Serious
- Grounded
- Non-academic
- Non-alarmist
- First-person plural where appropriate

### Semantic Positioning

Opening hook. Establishes emotional legitimacy before intellectual argument. Sets the register: serious, grounded, non-alarmist.

### Transformational Role

Transform private anxiety into civilizational perception.

### Systemic Relevance

Entry point to the whole book. If the reader does not feel recognized here, the rest of the argument will feel abstract.

### Claude Prompt Seed

Open with a scene or atmospheric observation that captures civilizational unease without naming it. Then name it. Then distinguish it from personal anxiety. Close by establishing that this feeling is the starting point of the book's inquiry.
