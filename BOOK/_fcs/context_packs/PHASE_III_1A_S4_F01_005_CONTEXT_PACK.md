## Module Specification
Module: F01-005
Title: Mobility: The Circulation of Bodies and Goods
Word target: 1,500–2,500 words
Role: Establish mobility as the circulatory function of civilization — the movement of people, goods, and materials that makes the metabolic system function.

## Must include
1. Mobility as circulation — not transport policy but the movement function of civilizational metabolism.
2. The evolution of mobility: foot, animal, sail, wheel, rail, automobile, aviation, digital.
3. The automobile civilization: how the car restructured habitat, infrastructure, and daily life.
4. Global supply chains as the circulatory system of industrial civilization.
5. The pathology: carbon emissions, congestion, inequality of access, supply chain fragility, aviation's climate impact.
6. The just-in-time fragility revealed by COVID-19 and supply chain crises.
7. Digital mobility: the movement of information as a new circulatory layer.
8. The regenerative direction: modal shift, electrification, supply chain redesign, local resilience, mobility as a service, slow mobility.
9. Bridge to Food.

## Hard constraints
- Do NOT reduce mobility to "transport policy."
- Do NOT ignore global supply chains.
- Do NOT ignore mobility inequality (access to mobility).
- Do NOT ignore digital mobility.

## Key idea
Mobility is not how people get around. It is the circulatory function through which civilization's metabolic flows are physically realized.

## Suggested closing direction
"But circulation requires something to circulate. The most fundamental of all civilizational flows — the one that sustains biological life itself — is food."
