## 5. Five Classes of Civilizational Flows (canonical — deploy fully)

For each flow class, include: (1) what the flow is, (2) what it enables, (3) how it becomes pathological, (4) one concise civilizational example, (5) how it interacts with the other flows.

**1. Energy Flows**
- Powers bodies, machines, infrastructure, computation, production, transport, communication
- Pathologies: fossil dependency, energy poverty, energetic overshoot, rebound effects, decarbonization without demand redesign
- Key idea: Energy is not just another sector. It is the metabolism's power supply.

**2. Material Flows**
- Physical substrates: minerals, metals, water, biomass, buildings, infrastructure, manufactured goods, waste streams
- Pathologies: linear extraction, landfill accumulation, plastic pollution, mining dependency, circularity failure
- Key idea: A civilization that cannot circulate materials eventually consumes its own material basis.

**3. Biological Flows**
- Food, soil, bodies, health, reproduction, ecosystems, biodiversity, regeneration
- Pathologies: soil depletion, biodiversity collapse, industrial food disease, public health fragility, ecosystem degradation
- Key idea: Biology is not external to civilization. It is one of civilization's life-support flows.

**4. Information Flows**
- Education, science, media, data, collective attention, governance signals, foresight, cultural transmission
- Pathologies: misinformation, information overload, attention capture, fragmented knowledge, institutional blindness, slow feedback loops
- Key idea: A civilization cannot regulate what it cannot perceive.

**5. Meaning Flows**
- Purpose, legitimacy, identity, ethics, belonging, worldview, civilizational direction
- Pathologies: nihilism, polarization, legitimacy collapse, meaninglessness, cultural fragmentation, loss of shared future
- Key idea: Meaning is not decorative. It is the orientation system of the civilizational metabolism.

## 6. Required Conceptual Distinction (MUST include)
The five flows tell us **what moves**.
The Seven Foundations will later tell us **through which civilizational organs** those flows are processed.
Do NOT collapse these layers.

## 7. Opening Direction (orientation)
To read a metabolism, one begins not with its parts but with its flows. What enters? What circulates? What is transformed? What accumulates? What fails to regenerate? A civilization may look powerful from the outside while its flows are becoming increasingly unviable from within.

## 8. Closing Direction (orientation)
The five flows make the metabolism visible. But flows do not move through empty space. They are captured, transformed, regulated, blocked, or regenerated by structures. The next task is to identify those structures: the civilizational organs through which the metabolism operates.
