## Module Specification
Module: F01-007
Title: Materials: The Substance of Civilization
Word target: 1,500–2,500 words
Role: Establish materials as the physical substance through which civilization builds and maintains its structures.

## Must include
1. Materials as the substance of civilization — not supply chains but the physical substrate of all built structures.
2. The material ages: stone, bronze, iron, steel, concrete, plastics, rare earths, composites.
3. Industrial material metabolism: extraction → processing → manufacturing → use → disposal.
4. The linear economy: take-make-waste as the dominant material paradigm.
5. The pathology: resource depletion, mining impacts, plastic pollution, e-waste, rare earth geopolitics, material inequality.
6. The circular economy as the regenerative direction: design for disassembly, closed-loop systems, material passports, urban mining.
7. Critical materials for the energy transition: lithium, cobalt, copper, silicon — the new material dependencies.
8. Materials and geopolitics: resource nationalism, supply chain concentration, strategic materials.
9. Bridge to F01-008 (Synthesis).

## Hard constraints
- Do NOT reduce materials to "supply chain management."
- Do NOT ignore the linear economy as the governing paradigm.
- Do NOT ignore rare earths and critical materials for energy transition.
- Do NOT ignore material inequality (who gets materials, who bears the costs).

## Key idea
Materials are not inputs to production. They are the physical substance through which civilization's metabolic structures are built, maintained, and renewed.

## Suggested closing direction
"The six flows — energy, water, habitat, infrastructure, mobility, food, materials — are not separate systems. They are facets of a single metabolic reality. The question is how they integrate, and what their integration reveals about the health or pathology of civilization as a whole."
