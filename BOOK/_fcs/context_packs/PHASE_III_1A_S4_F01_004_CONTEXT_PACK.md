## Module Specification
Module: F01-004
Title: Infrastructure: The Connective Tissue
Word target: 1,500–2,500 words
Role: Establish infrastructure as the connective tissue that links all civilizational flows.

## Must include
1. Infrastructure as connective tissue — not a sector but the integration layer of civilization.
2. The major infrastructure systems: energy grids, water networks, transportation networks, digital networks, sanitation, logistics.
3. Infrastructure and civilizational complexity: the more complex the civilization, the more infrastructure it requires.
4. Infrastructure lock-in: the physical path dependency created by large infrastructure investments.
5. The maintenance crisis: aging infrastructure, deferred maintenance, the infrastructure gap.
6. Digital infrastructure as the newest connective tissue — internet, data centers, cloud, undersea cables.
7. Infrastructure inequality: the infrastructure gap between global north and south, urban and rural.
8. The pathology: brittle optimization, single points of failure, cascading failures, climate vulnerability.
9. The regenerative direction: resilient infrastructure design, distributed systems, modular architecture, climate-adaptive infrastructure, infrastructure as ecosystem.
10. Bridge to Mobility.

## Hard constraints
- Do NOT reduce infrastructure to "roads and bridges."
- Do NOT ignore digital infrastructure.
- Do NOT ignore the global south and infrastructure inequality.
- Do NOT make this a policy chapter.

## Key idea
Infrastructure is not the background of civilization. It is the connective tissue through which all flows become coordinated action.

## Suggested closing direction
"But infrastructure connects fixed points. What moves between them — the bodies, goods, and materials in circulation — is the domain of mobility."
