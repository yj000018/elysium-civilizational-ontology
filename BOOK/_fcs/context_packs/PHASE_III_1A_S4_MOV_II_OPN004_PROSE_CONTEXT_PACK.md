## Current Module: OPN-004

**Canonical title:** The Illusion of Silos

**Target:** 700 words. Acceptable range: 600–850 words.

**Purpose:** Show that fragmented domains create an illusion of separability. The patient cannot be diagnosed if each organ/system is treated as unrelated.

**Reader transition:**
- State in: Civilization is the patient.
- State out: The reader sees that our maps divide the patient into silos, making whole diagnosis structurally difficult.

**Tone:** clear, diagnostic, respectful of expertise, non-polemical, precise

**Beats:**
1. Define the silo: a domain of expertise with clear internal vision and blind periphery
2. Show 2–3 concrete examples of silo blindness
3. Distinguish silo blindness from ignorance or incompetence
4. Show how silos interact: each optimizes locally, creating global dysfunction
5. Bridge to OPN-005: partial solutions

**Key points:**
- Honor expertise — silos are not stupid
- Critique fragmentation, not intelligence
- Structural blindness is systemic, not individual
- Local optimization creates global dysfunction

**Closing direction:** Prepare OPN-005 by showing that when silos dominate, solutions often move pressure elsewhere.
