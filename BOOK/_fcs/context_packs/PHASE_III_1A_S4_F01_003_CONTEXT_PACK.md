## Module Specification
Module: F01-003
Title: Habitat: The Organized Space
Word target: 1,500–2,500 words
Role: Establish habitat as the organized space within which civilizational metabolism occurs.

## Must include
1. Habitat as organized space — both built environment and natural environment.
2. The evolution of human habitat: nomadic, village, city, metropolis, megacity.
3. The city as the primary habitat of industrial civilization.
4. Urban metabolism: flows of energy, water, food, materials, waste through cities.
5. The pathology: urban sprawl, informal settlements, ecological destruction, climate vulnerability, housing crisis.
6. The relationship between built habitat and natural habitat — not opposition but interdependence.
7. Ecological habitat: biodiversity, ecosystem services, the living substrate beneath the built environment.
8. The regenerative direction: biophilic design, urban ecology, compact cities, ecological corridors, habitat restoration, climate-resilient design.
9. Bridge to Infrastructure.

## Hard constraints
- Do NOT reduce habitat to "housing policy."
- Do NOT ignore ecological habitat (non-human).
- Do NOT make this a real estate or urban planning chapter.
- Do NOT ignore informal settlements and the global south.

## Key idea
Habitat is not where civilization lives. It is the organized space through which civilization metabolizes.

## Suggested closing direction
"But organized space requires connection. The habitat cannot function in isolation — it must be linked, supplied, and integrated through the connective tissue of infrastructure."
