## Module Specification
Module: F01-002
Title: Water: The Circulatory Constraint
Word target: 1,500–2,500 words
Role: Establish water as the irreplaceable circulatory medium of civilization.

## Must include
1. Water as the medium of all biological and civilizational processes — not a resource but a circulatory medium.
2. The hydrological cycle as the planetary circulatory system.
3. Historical civilizations and their water systems (irrigation, aqueducts, hydraulic societies).
4. Industrial civilization's water metabolism: extraction, treatment, distribution, sewage, industrial use.
5. The pathology: depletion of aquifers, contamination, disruption of the hydrological cycle, inequitable access.
6. Climate change and water: intensification of extremes (flood/drought), glacial retreat, altered precipitation.
7. Virtual water and embedded water in food and manufactured goods.
8. The regenerative direction: watershed restoration, circular water systems, demand reduction, ecological water allocation, water sovereignty.
9. Bridge to Habitat.

## Hard constraints
- Do NOT reduce water to a "resource scarcity" narrative.
- Do NOT ignore the hydrological cycle as the governing frame.
- Do NOT make this a development/aid chapter.
- Do NOT ignore industrial and agricultural water use.

## Key idea
Water is not a resource. It is the circulatory medium through which civilization's metabolism flows.

## Suggested closing direction
"Water moves through everything. But it must move through somewhere — through the organized spaces that human civilization has built and continues to build. The next foundation is Habitat."
