## Module Specification
Module: F01-006
Title: Food: The Nutritive Flow
Word target: 1,500–2,500 words
Role: Establish food as the nutritive flow that sustains biological life within civilizational metabolism.

## Must include
1. Food as the nutritive flow — not agriculture policy but the biological-civilizational interface.
2. The agricultural revolution as the founding act of civilization.
3. Industrial agriculture: the Green Revolution, synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, monocultures, factory farming.
4. The food system as a metabolic subsystem: soil → seed → grow → harvest → process → distribute → consume → waste.
5. The pathology: soil degradation, biodiversity loss, nitrogen cycle disruption, food waste, obesity/malnutrition paradox, food sovereignty erosion.
6. The climate-food nexus: agriculture as both driver and victim of climate change.
7. Food and culture: food as identity, ritual, and civilizational memory.
8. The regenerative direction: agroecology, regenerative agriculture, food sovereignty, urban food systems, reduced meat, circular food systems.
9. Bridge to Materials.

## Hard constraints
- Do NOT reduce food to "food security" or "hunger."
- Do NOT ignore industrial agriculture's role in ecological crisis.
- Do NOT make this a nutrition chapter.
- Do NOT ignore food culture and identity.

## Key idea
Food is not what civilization eats. It is the nutritive flow through which biological life is maintained within civilizational metabolism.

## Suggested closing direction
"But nutrition requires substance. The material flows that civilization extracts, transforms, and deploys — the metals, minerals, plastics, and fibers — constitute the next dimension of material existence."
