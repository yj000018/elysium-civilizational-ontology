# ELYSIUM Book Part Architecture

**Status:** CANONICAL
**Phase:** Phase III-0B
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-27

## Purpose
This document defines the high-level structural parts of the ELYSIUM manuscript.

## Part 01: Opening (The Diagnosis and The Map)
- **Function:** Hook the reader, diagnose the meta-crisis, and introduce the ELYSIUM ontology as the necessary lens for understanding.
- **Scope:** The failure of siloed thinking, the concept of civilization as a metabolic entity, and a high-level overview of the 7 foundations.
- **Estimated Modules:** 3–6

## Part 02: Foundations (The Anatomy of Civilization)
- **Function:** The core of the book. A detailed, sequential exploration of the 7 foundations and their facets.
- **Scope:** 
  - F01 Material Base
  - F02 Vitality
  - F03 Agency
  - F04 Cohesion
  - F05 Governance
  - F06 Vision
  - F07 Consciousness
- **Estimated Modules:** 3–8 per foundation.

## Part 03: Transition Dynamics (The Physiology of Change)
- **Function:** To explain how the foundations interact, how systemic lock-in occurs, and where the leverage points for transformation exist.
- **Scope:** Feedback loops, phase shifts, the mechanics of collapse vs. regeneration.
- **Estimated Modules:** 4–8

## Part 04: Implementation (The Architecture of the New Society)
- **Function:** To make the ontology actionable for builders, policymakers, and leaders.
- **Scope:** Practical application of the ELYSIUM framework to real-world design, policy, and organizational structure.
- **Estimated Modules:** 4–8

## Part 05: Appendices
- **Function:** To house methodological details, reference material, and the formal matrix, keeping the main narrative clean.
- **Scope:** The 12-Step Universal Analysis Matrix in full detail, Index of the 126 Integrated Models, Glossary.

## Decisions Made
- The 12-step matrix will *inform* the writing of Part 02, but will not be used as a rigid, visible template for every facet, to preserve narrative flow. The formal matrix will reside in the Appendices.

## Open Questions
- Should Part 04 (Implementation) be structured by domain (e.g., "For the Entrepreneur", "For the Policymaker") or by systemic challenge?
