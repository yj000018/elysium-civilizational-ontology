# ELYSIUM Book Chapter (Foundation) Model

**Status:** CANONICAL
**Phase:** Phase III-0B
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-27

## Purpose
This document defines how a "Chapter" (which corresponds to a Foundation, e.g., F01 Material Base) is structured within the manuscript.

## Structural Role
A Chapter/Foundation is a major conceptual container. It groups related facets and provides the overarching narrative for that layer of the civilizational stack.

## Standard Chapter Components
Every Foundation Chapter must contain:

1. **The Executive Summary / Opening:** A compelling introduction that defines the foundation's role in the civilizational metabolism.
2. **The Reader Promise:** What the reader will understand differently after reading this chapter.
3. **The Narrative Modules:** The actual prose sections (modules) that explore the facets. These modules weave the facets together rather than treating them as isolated encyclopedia entries.
4. **The Transition Vector:** How this specific foundation must shift from its current pathological state to a regenerative state.

## Relationship to Facets
Facets (e.g., Energy, Water, Habitat within F01) are the *conceptual ingredients* of the chapter. The chapter does not need a rigid "Section 1: Energy, Section 2: Water" structure if a more integrated narrative (e.g., "The Nexus of Energy and Water in Urban Habitat") serves the argument better. However, all canonical facets assigned to the foundation must be thoroughly covered.

## Matrix Coverage Strategy
The 12-Step Universal Analysis Matrix provides the analytical rigor. The chapter must ensure that across its modules, it addresses the current state, the pathologies, the models, and the regenerative future for its facets, without explicitly listing the 12 steps as headings.
